Getting Started

Today you’ll get three quick wins: rewrite something, summarize something, and plan next steps. Use the small prompt recipes below and keep your asks short.

What you’ll learn

  • Use a four-line recipe that keeps your first prompts clear and direct.
  • Ship three quick wins—rewrite, summarize, and plan—so you build momentum fast.
  • Capture the prompts and results you like so you can reuse them later.

The simplest way to ask

Use this mini-recipe whenever you talk to ChatGPT:

Goal: <what you want>
Audience: <who it's for>
Rules: <1–2 short rules>
Format: <email, bullets, table, etc.>

You’ll see more patterns in Prompt Basics.

Step-by-step

  1. Open ChatGPT and pin the recipe. Keep the four lines visible in a note or the chat.
  2. Run Win #1 — Rewrite. Paste a rough paragraph and request a clearer version.
  3. Run Win #2 — Summarize. Share short notes and ask for key points plus a takeaway.
  4. Run Win #3 — Plan. Describe a tiny goal and request timed steps.
  5. Save what you liked. Store the prompts and answers in your notes for reuse.

Win #1 — Rewrite (5 minutes)

  1. Paste a rough paragraph or email.
  2. Ask for a cleaner version with the recipe.
Goal: Rewrite this email to be friendly and clear  
Audience: coworker who asked for a status update  
Rules: keep it under 120 words; keep any dates  
Format: email draft

<paste your email here>

Win #2 — Summarize (5 minutes)

  1. Paste a short article or notes (or describe it briefly).
  2. Ask for key points and a two-sentence takeaway.
Goal: Summarize the key points and give me a 2-sentence takeaway  
Audience: me  
Rules: no hype; only what the text says  
Format: 5 bullets + takeaway

<paste text or give a link/description>

When facts matter, learn quick checks in Source Quality & Fact Checking.

Win #3 — Plan (5 minutes)

  1. Name a small goal (travel day, study plan, chore list).
  2. Ask for steps with time estimates.
Goal: Make a simple step-by-step plan to <your goal>  
Audience: me  
Rules: realistic time estimates; 6 steps max  
Format: numbered list with durations

Save what works

  • Copy the best answers you get into a notes file.
  • Save the exact prompt that worked so you can reuse it.
  • Keep a short list of “go-to prompts” you trust.

Tips & pitfalls

  • Tip: Keep each prompt under 150 words—the model responds faster and stays on topic.
  • Tip: Add a one-line audience cue (e.g., “for a new teammate”) to improve tone instantly.
  • Pitfall: Asking for everything at once. Split complex requests into the three wins first.
  • Pitfall: Forgetting to respond when it’s close. Say the fix in one sentence before retrying.
