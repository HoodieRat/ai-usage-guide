Prerequisites

  • Platform with permissions, search, and version history.
  • Information architecture draft with maintainers per section.
  • Templates for case studies, mitigations, and incident reports.

Step-by-step

  1. Choose platform. Notion, Confluence, or SharePoint with export options.
  2. Design IA. Onboarding, prompts, evaluations, incidents, governance.
  3. Publish templates. Case study, mitigation guide, incident report pages.

Output contract

Your KB should expose:

  • IA map: sections, owners, and review cadence.
  • Page templates: reusable structures for common content types.
  • Taxonomy: tags, synonyms, and naming conventions.
  • Governance: change control, archival policy, and access rules.

Logging

Metrics that matter

  • Search success rate: % of searches that end on a relevant page.
  • Freshness: % of pages reviewed within SLA.
  • Adoption: unique viewers and referenced-by counts.

Troubleshooting

Out-of-date pages.
Escalate to owners; auto-tag and schedule reviews.
Hard to find content.
Simplify tags; add synonyms and TL;DR blocks.
Low adoption.
Embed the KB in workflows; link from briefs and PR templates.

Recap

A living knowledge base reduces repeat questions and speeds onboarding. Keep ownership clear and reviews on a cadence.
