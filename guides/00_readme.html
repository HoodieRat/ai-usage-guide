Read Me First

Welcome to the Everyday AI Guide orientation. In one sitting you will map your learning path, earn a quick win, and capture the guardrails that keep every future session safe.

What you’ll learn

  • How the Everyday AI Guide is structured so you can choose the right unit to tackle next.
  • How to run the 15-minute Quickstart and log the outcome in your run journal.
  • Which foundational guides—Getting Started and How ChatGPT Works—to review before you dive deeper.

Orientation roadmap

  1. Quick win (15 minutes): Use the starter prompt below on a real task and ship the result.
  2. Set up your workspace: Spend 10 minutes in Getting Started to configure access, custom instructions, and your run log template.
  3. Understand the model: Read How ChatGPT Works so you know how tokens, context windows, and verification loops behave.
  4. Queue the Core Skills track: Work through Prompt Basics, Structured Output, Add Your Files, Check with the Web, and Verify Before You Share in order.

Step-by-step

  1. Choose a tiny, real task. Example: rewrite an email, draft meeting notes, or outline a checklist.
  2. Collect the essentials. Note the audience, success criteria, and any inputs you will paste so the assistant stays focused.
  3. Prime ChatGPT. Open the app, paste the starter prompt below, and replace each placeholder with your details.
  4. Ship a first result. Review the draft, request a targeted revision if anything is off, and iterate once.
  5. Log the run. Store the prompt, answer, and lessons in your run journal so you can reuse or audit later.
  6. Lock in your next guides. Block time for Getting Started and How ChatGPT Works before you move to the next unit.

Starter Prompt (copy-paste)

You are helping me finish a small deliverable.

Goal: <what do you want? one sentence>
Context: <audience, key facts, links, or constraints>
Rules: Be concise. No filler. Flag risks or missing info before drafting.
Output format: <bullet list, email draft, table, etc.>

If you need more details, ask in one line. Otherwise produce the deliverable and add a short summary of your approach.

Quick wins to practice

  • Rewrite: Paste rough text, target a specific audience, and compare the before/after in your run log.
  • Summarize: Drop in meeting notes and request key points plus two recommended follow-up actions.
  • Plan: Ask for a short checklist with estimated effort so you can schedule the work.

Tips & pitfalls

  • Tip: Lead with goal, audience, and format. The assistant does better when context arrives before the ask.
  • Tip: Review the context window guidance in How ChatGPT Works so you know when to reset a thread.
  • Pitfall: Dropping huge info dumps. Break large inputs into focused chunks for higher accuracy.
  • Pitfall: Skipping your run log. Without notes you cannot reuse prompts or prove how a result was created.

What’s next

  • Understand tokens, context limits, and verification loops in How ChatGPT Works.
  • Continue through Prompt Basics and Structured Output to build repeatable workflows.
