Deep Research Playbook

Coordinate multi-week investigations in ChatGPT web chat by combining scoped questions, retrieval workflows, evidence QA, and synthesis checkpoints. Everything here assumes the browsing tool is available and you’re keeping logs in your project folder.

As of October 30, 2025: Re-verify high-impact claims within 48 hours of publication. Stats, policies, and market figures shift frequently. See Browsing & Search.
ChatGPT-specific: Long chats can slow down and drop context. Follow Sessions & Context to recap and rehydrate into a new thread. Use the handoff brief from Handoff Between Chats when teammates take over.

What you’ll learn

  • How to design AI-assisted research with clear roles, sub-questions, and milestones.
  • Evidence logging habits that hold up to stakeholder, editorial, and compliance review.
  • Synthesis patterns that surface insights, conflicts, and remaining gaps—cited and auditable.

Prerequisites

  • A brief describing objective, decision date, and success metrics.
  • An evidence workspace (sheet/db) in your Project Folders (e.g., 10_inputs/research + 30_working-drafts/research-log.md).
  • Source quality rubric and citation rules (pair with Citations & Attribution).

Planner → Actor → Reviewer walkthrough

Planner prompt

“Design a research roadmap for evaluating AI vendor risk in 2025. Break into sub-questions, recommend primary/secondary sources, propose a weekly review cadence, and list milestone deliverables.”

Actor response

ChatGPT decomposes the work, drafts a research matrix, and proposes queries and source tiers for each sub-question.

Reviewer check

Reviewer adds compliance checkpoints, requires inline citations with confidence labels, and requests a milestone synthesis prompt.

Step-by-step

  1. Frame the problem. Start a new browsing-enabled chat. Paste the charter: question, scope (time/geo), definitions, and “done” criteria. Link your evidence log file path.
  2. Decompose. Ask for sub-questions, target sources, and owners. Approve the list before searching.
  3. Collect evidence. Run searches per sub-question. For each source, require title, author, publisher, date, jurisdiction, short quote (≤25 words), and an ID (e.g., SRC-203).
  4. Validate & compare. Use a reviewer prompt to re-open each source, confirm the quote, and log conflicts or caveats.
  5. Synthesize. At each milestone, have ChatGPT cluster findings, cite sources, highlight conflicts, and list decisions / next steps.
  6. Rehydrate often. When the thread gets heavy, paste the latest recap and evidence table into a fresh chat and continue.

Output contract (required at every checkpoint)

  • Sub-question status: Open / In progress / Resolved / Blocked, with owner & due date.
  • Evidence table: Source ID, claim text, short quote, confidence (H/M/L) with rationale, link/path, verification owner.
  • Insights summary: 3–5 synthesized insights with inline [SRC-###].
  • Conflict & gap log: What disagrees or is missing, impact, and next action.
  • Next actions: AI vs human tasks, deadlines, and acceptance criteria.

Copy & paste assets

1) Research charter template

# Research Charter
Question: {{core question}}
Scope: {{date window, regions, industries}}
Definitions: {{key terms}}
Decision date: {{yyyy-mm-dd}}
Success: {{what decision this research will enable}}
Deliverable: {{format + audience}}
Evidence log: 10_inputs/research/research-log.md

2) Sub-question builder (ask ChatGPT)

Break the charter into 6–10 sub-questions. For each include:
- Query plan (Boolean/site/filetype)
- Expected source tier (Primary/Secondary/Commentary)
- Evidence type (stat, policy, quote, dataset)
- Owner and due date
Return a table.

3) Evidence table (paste in your log)

| Source ID | URL/Path | Title | Author/Publisher | Date | Jurisdiction | Claim/Stat | Quote (≤25w) | Confidence | Verified by | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|------------------|------|--------------|------------|--------------|------------|------------|-------|
| SRC-201 | https://... | ... | ... | 2025-09-12 | US | ... | "..." | High | Dana | PDF saved |

4) Reviewer QA prompt

Re-open each source in the evidence table. For every row:
- Confirm author & publication date
- Extract a ≤25-word supporting quote + page/section
- Rate confidence with rationale (primary/secondary/age)
- Flag conflicts/paywalls/dead links and suggest substitutes
Return: Source ID | Passed (Y/N) | Issues | Next action

5) Milestone synthesis prompt

You are producing a milestone synthesis.
Inputs: {{latest evidence rows}} and {{conflict log}}
Tasks:
1) Cluster findings into 3–6 themes with inline citations [SRC-###].
2) List the top conflicts/gaps and propose resolution steps.
3) Provide a decision-ready summary (≤200 words) for stakeholders.
4) Next actions split by AI vs human with deadlines.
Return markdown sections.

6) Rehydrate / handoff snippet (for new chat or teammate)

Context transfer (condensed):
- Project: {{name}} — decision by {{date}}
- Objective & definitions: {{1–2 lines}}
- Current status: {{open vs resolved sub-questions}}
- Evidence log path: 10_inputs/research/research-log.md
- Last synthesis link: {{message URL or file}}
- Next actions: {{list}}
Acknowledge by restating objective, risks, and your first two steps.

Logging

Store evidence tables and synthesis briefs in the project folder. Download key web pages as PDFs/screenshots. Paste message links to the citation list so reviewers can re-open the exact ChatGPT responses that produced them.

Metrics that matter

  • Sub-question velocity: % of sub-questions resolved per sprint.
  • Verification coverage: % of claims with reviewer sign-off.
  • Conflict closure time: Days from contradiction to resolved note.
  • Refresh compliance: % of high-impact claims re-verified within 48 hours of ship.

Tips & pitfalls

  • Tip: Start each milestone review by having ChatGPT restate the charter; park any off-scope findings in a separate tab.
  • Tip: Evaluate evidence quality with a quick rubric—primary, secondary, commentary—and note confidence rationale in the log.
  • Pitfall: Recycling the same synthesis each week. Run a novelty check and highlight what changed in confidence or direction.
  • Pitfall: Letting threads drag on. Rehydrate into a fresh chat whenever latency spikes and link back to Sessions & Context for the recap pattern.

Recap

Deep research in ChatGPT works when you structure the work: charter → sub-questions → evidence → verification → synthesis → rehydrate. Keep citations tight, conflicts visible, and decisions on schedule.

Acceptance checklist

  • ✅ Charter approved and stored in the project folder.
  • ✅ Sub-question matrix with owners and due dates.
  • ✅ Evidence table with citations, quotes, and confidence labels.
  • ✅ Reviewer QA run; conflicts logged with actions.
  • ✅ Milestone synthesis delivered and rehydration snippet prepared.
