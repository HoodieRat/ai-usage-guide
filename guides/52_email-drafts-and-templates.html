Email Drafts & Templates

Turn ChatGPT into your email co-writer by codifying tone, guardrails, and review loops so messages stay on brand and compliant.

Related guides: Use alongside Response Contracts, File Upload Best Practices (for attachments), and Documentation Handoffs for distribution.
Guardrails: Ban copying confidential info into prompts. For regulated industries, route drafts through an approver before sending externally.

What you’ll learn

  • Designing reusable email briefs that capture tone, audience, and call to action.
  • Prompting ChatGPT to generate drafts that respect formatting and compliance rules.
  • Building an email template library with ownership and refresh cadences.

Prerequisites

  • Voice & tone guidelines plus any legal disclaimers required for your organization.
  • Access to a shared template repository (docs, CMS, or email platform).
  • Review checklist covering factual accuracy, personalization, and opt-out language.

Step-by-step

  1. Collect inputs. Capture sender, recipient segments, objective, supporting facts, and any links or attachments you will reference.
  2. Load the response contract. Provide ChatGPT with the required structure (subject, preview, body sections, CTA) and tone guardrails (e.g., friendly, concise, 130 words max).
  3. Generate the draft. Prompt for one primary draft and a variant; ask for highlighted merge fields ({{first_name}}, {{product}}) so you spot personalization gaps.
  4. Run the compliance check. Have ChatGPT or a second reviewer confirm required disclaimers, opt-out language, and factual claims using your checklist.
  5. Finalize the template. Apply edits, add placeholders for dynamic content, and store the template with version number, owner, and last review date.
  6. Publish and distribute. Upload to the email platform or shared template doc; tag it by audience, lifecycle stage, and risk level.
  7. Capture learnings. After sending, log performance (open/reply rate) and note any manual fixes so the template evolves over time.

Output contract

Each approved template should include:

  • Template metadata: Audience, objective, owner, last review date, and compliance status.
  • Draft plus variants: Primary email copy with merge fields and at least one alternate intro or CTA.
  • Checklist snapshot: Completed review checklist with sign-off initials.
  • Performance notes: Latest send metrics and recommended follow-up prompts.

Return merge fields in double curly braces.

Review checklist snippet

- ☐ Subject matches policy and avoids spam phrases
- ☐ All metrics or claims cite a source
- ☐ Personalization fields present and confirmed
- ☐ CTA links correct and tracked
- ☐ Required disclaimer appended

Logging

Track template name, owner, version, last review date, approver, and links to the ChatGPT conversation or prompt pack. Store change rationale when edits go live.

Metrics that matter

  • Approval turnaround: Time from draft generation to sign-off.
  • Personalization coverage: Percentage of templates with validated merge fields.
  • Performance lift: Open or reply rate improvement versus the prior baseline.

Tips & pitfalls

  • Document forbidden phrases: Maintain a list of banned words (spam triggers, legal issues) and include it in the prompt to reduce rework.
  • Keep variants small: Limit to two versions per review cycle so approvers can actually read them.
  • Start with plain text: Approve the copy before layering on HTML modules; it keeps reviews fast and accessible.
  • Refresh quarterly: Schedule a template audit every 90 days to retire underperforming copy and capture new positioning.

Recap

Guide ChatGPT with crystal-clear response contracts, double-check compliance, and treat templates like living assets so every send stays sharp and on brand.

Acceptance checklist

  • ✅ Draft follows tone, structure, and CTA requirements.
  • ✅ Review checklist signed and stored with the template.
  • ✅ Template lives in the shared library with owner and next review date.
  • ✅ Performance notes logged after each send cycle.
