Meetings and Agendas

Keep AI-related meetings short, purposeful, and documented with clear outcomes.

Cross-links: Prep work with Collaboration Workflows, define roles via Task Chains, and store minutes in Knowledge Bases.
Accessibility: Share agendas 24 hours ahead and provide transcripts or notes for teammates who can’t attend.

What you’ll learn

  • Designing agendas that balance demos, decisions, and feedback.
  • Capturing meeting outputs so action items flow into execution tools.
  • Leveraging AI to prepare, facilitate, and follow up.
  • Running retros that refine the cadence and agenda template over time.

Prerequisites

  • Shared doc with agenda template and decision log.
  • Owner roles assigned: facilitator, timekeeper, note-taker.
  • Recording/transcription enabled where policy permits.

Step-by-step

  1. Clarify the purpose. Decide whether the session is for decisions, alignment, or ideation and timebox accordingly.
  2. Draft the agenda. Use AI to outline goals, presenters, materials, and expected outputs.
  3. Distribute pre-work. Send agenda and context at least 24 hours ahead with explicit asks.
  4. Facilitate intentionally. Assign roles and use AI to surface prior decisions or metrics on demand.
  5. Capture outcomes live. Log decisions, owners, and due dates in the shared tracker before ending.
  6. Follow through. Within 24 hours, publish minutes, link tickets/prompts, and schedule follow-ups.

Output contract

Every meeting should produce:

  • Minutes: decisions, owners, due dates, and links to artifacts.
  • Action register: list of tasks with status and next review date.
  • Recording/transcript: or human-edited summary when recording is not allowed.

AI follow-up prompt

Summarize the meeting notes into: (1) decisions, (2) actions with owners/dates, (3) open questions.
Use bullet lists; keep under 200 words. Add links found in the notes.

Logging

Store meeting minutes, decision log entries, and action items in the shared workspace. Include a unique meeting ID and link to the recording or summary.

Metrics that matter

  • Decision rate: # of decisions per meeting.
  • Action completion: % of tasks completed by the next check-in.
  • Time discipline: % of agenda items that stayed within timebox.

Troubleshooting

Discussions run long.
Strict timeboxing; move deep dives to async docs.
Low participation.
Rotate roles; send pre-work with explicit asks.
Outcomes not recorded.
Close every meeting by reading back owners and due dates.

Recap

Short, purposeful meetings align humans; AI prepares and follows through. Capture decisions and owners before the call ends.

Acceptance checklist

  • ✅ Agenda and materials sent 24h ahead.
  • ✅ Roles assigned and visible.
  • ✅ Decisions and actions logged with owners/dates.
  • ✅ Minutes published and linked to the knowledge base.
