Troubleshooting Bad Answers

Turn disappointing outputs into learning loops—rapidly diagnose misses, recover signal, and know when to escalate or stop.

Signal first: Capture the bad answer, your prompt, and surrounding context immediately so you can compare iterations.

Stop conditions: If you detect legal, compliance, or safety risk, halt the session and escalate instead of iterating.

What you’ll learn

  • Recognizing patterns behind weak responses (missing context, hallucinations, policy blocks).
  • Applying structured retry prompts that target the root cause.
  • Capturing diagnostics and routing escalations to SMEs or safety teams.
  • Shaping follow-up questions that teach the model what “good” looks like.

Prerequisites

  • Saved prompt history or transcript logging for the workflow.
  • Access to policy guidance for sensitive domains.
  • Checklist of acceptable sources or correctness tests for your domain.

Step-by-step

  1. Capture evidence. Save the raw prompt, response, and any files involved. Redact sensitive data before sharing internally.
  2. Classify the miss. Determine whether the issue is accuracy, completeness, format, tone, or policy refusal.
  3. Diagnose root cause. Ask ChatGPT to explain how it reached the answer. Compare against your sources to highlight specific gaps or errors.
  4. Retry with constraints. Provide corrected context, cite required sources, and set success criteria (e.g., “Reference policy section 4.2”).
  5. Validate externally. Confirm revised outputs against authoritative sources or automated tests. If still wrong, escalate to a human SME.
  6. Decide to stop or hand off. If the risk is high or retries exceed three attempts, stop and notify the safety contact with logs.
  7. Update playbooks. Record the scenario, prompts, and final resolution in your troubleshooting library to prevent repeats.

Output contract

  • Every retry includes a documented hypothesis and expected fix.
  • Final answers cite sources or pass automated tests before release.
  • Escalations follow the incident intake path with timestamps and owners.
  • Lessons learned feed back into team prompt libraries or guardrails.

Metrics that matter

  • Fix velocity: Median time from bad answer to approved resolution.
  • Retry count: Average number of iterations before getting a correct response.
  • Escalation rate: Percentage of sessions requiring SME intervention.

Tips & pitfalls

  • Use controlled retries: Change one variable at a time so you know what fixed the issue.
  • Challenge assumptions: Ask the model what could make its answer wrong to expose blind spots.
  • Watch for policy triggers: Hidden safety filters may cause evasive responses—acknowledge compliance needs explicitly.
  • Close the loop: Share resolved cases in weekly reviews to reinforce collective learning.

Recap

When ChatGPT stumbles, disciplined diagnosis and escalation keep your workflows accurate and safe.

Acceptance checklist

  • ✅ Troubleshooting log captures prompts, attempts, and resolutions.
  • ✅ Retry prompts follow the root-cause framework above.
  • ✅ Escalation paths documented and rehearsed.
  • ✅ Lessons learned circulated to the broader team.
