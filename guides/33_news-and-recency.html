News & Recency

Stay current without sacrificing accuracy by establishing routines for verifying dates, sources, and breaking developments.

As of today (October 30, 2025): Time-sensitive information can change hourly. Note the retrieval timestamp for every claim and re-verify before publication. Pair this page with Source Quality & Fact Checking and Citations & Attribution.
New here? Read Browsing & Search for safe web lookups and Structured Output to capture timestamps and outlets in a consistent format.

What you’ll learn

  • When and how to trigger recency checks during research and delivery.
  • How to compare publish dates vs. event dates and handle corrections.
  • How to document verification cadence so stakeholders know freshness limits.

Prerequisites

  • Approved source roster for breaking news, regulatory updates, and company statements.
  • Shared tracking sheet or dashboard that records time-stamped updates.
  • Alert subscriptions (RSS, push, email) configured for critical keywords.

Planner → Actor → Reviewer walkthrough

Planner prompt

“Stand up a monitoring plan for semiconductor policy news this week. Include source tiers, refresh cadence, and escalation rules.”

Actor response

The assistant drafts a coverage calendar, recommends verified feeds, and outlines how AI summaries will be verified before sharing.

Reviewer check

Reviewer confirms priority topics, adds mandatory human review for high-stakes items, and sets the cadence for stakeholder digests.

Step-by-step

  1. Define scope & volatility. Tag topics:
    • High: re-check every 2–6 hours.
    • Medium: re-check daily.
    • Low: re-check weekly or on change.
  2. Monitor feeds. Use AI browsing plus trusted wires, official channels, and expert newsletters; capture publication and event timestamps for each update.
  3. Triangulate updates. Require at least two independent confirmations for publish-ready claims; log gaps or conflicts.
  4. Publish recency notes. Embed “Verified as of” statements and link to the monitoring log for transparency.
  5. Retire stale data. Set reminders to remove or refresh outdated claims so briefs remain current.

Output contract

Every news-driven deliverable must include:

  • Timestamp banner: “Verified as of YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM [TZ]” plus next review time.
  • Source ledger: Table of updates with event date, publish date, outlet, URL/title, and verification status.
  • Volatility flag: Indicator showing high-risk items that require more frequent checks.
  • Escalation log: Record of alerts escalated to legal, comms, or leadership.

Sunset checklist

- Remove updates older than the volatility threshold unless still relevant.
- Replace or annotate superseded stats with "superseded on {{date}}".
- Notify stakeholders that prior versions are archived.

Logging

Log every monitoring session with start time, feeds consulted, notable updates, who acknowledged changes, and links to saved evidence. Store the monitor file in your shared project space so successors can pick up without gaps.

For research hygiene, follow Browsing & Search and record retrieval timestamps for each source.

Metrics that matter

  • Refresh adherence: % of required checks executed on schedule.
  • Verification lag: Time from first detection to confirmed status.
  • Stale data incidents: Count of deliverables that used outdated information.

Tips & pitfalls

  • Tip: Confirm breaking updates with at least one official source before sharing summaries.
  • Tip: Always log both event and publish timestamps using absolute dates (e.g., “2025-10-30 17:20 MDT”).
  • Pitfall: Relying on a single outlet. Track two independent confirmations for every high-volatility claim.
  • Pitfall: Forgetting recency maintenance. Schedule calendar or bot reminders and assign backups so checks never lapse.
  • Pitfall: Letting conflicting timestamps slide. Note discrepancies in the ledger and pursue corrections before publishing.

Recap

Accurate news workflows balance speed with verification. Track updates with timestamps, confirm through trusted outlets, and publish clear recency disclosures.

Acceptance checklist

  • ✅ Monitoring scope defined with volatility tiers and owners.
  • ✅ Recency monitor populated with event/publish timestamps and verification status.
  • ✅ Stakeholder deliverables include “Verified as of” disclosures.
  • ✅ Follow-up reminders scheduled for each high-volatility item.
