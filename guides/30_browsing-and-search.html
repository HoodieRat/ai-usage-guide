Research

Browsing & Search

Use ChatGPT’s browsing to find fresh facts, compare viewpoints, and capture citations you can defend. This play keeps searches scoped, sources diverse, and logs audit-ready.

As of October 30, 2025: Policies and stats change fast. When you browse with ChatGPT, require author, publication date, and jurisdiction for any claim you intend to ship.
ChatGPT-specific: Start a new chat with browsing enabled for time-sensitive topics. If the session gets long or sluggish, handoff/rehydrate into a fresh chat and bring a recap so the model doesn’t lose context mid-research.

What you’ll learn

  • How to scope research questions, time bounds, and authority tiers inside ChatGPT.
  • Prompt patterns that force diverse, reputable sources (gov, academia, standards bodies).
  • Logging habits that turn ad-hoc browsing into an auditable research trail.

Prerequisites

  • Browsing-enabled ChatGPT model (for static topics, you can still use non-browsing + uploaded sources).
  • A research log (sheet or doc) stored in your project folder.
  • Source quality rubric (pair with Source Quality & Fact Checking).

Planner → Actor → Reviewer walkthrough

Planner prompt

“Find fresh stats on remote work adoption in 2025 for North America and EU. List queries to run, acceptable source tiers (primary, secondary), and how we’ll log results.”

Actor response

ChatGPT proposes query templates, prioritizes government/official statistics and reputable research, and drafts a logging table with URL, author, date, and verification owner.

Reviewer check

Reviewer runs a validation pass asking ChatGPT to re-open each source, extract author/date, check for conflicts, and mark any paywalled or stale links.

Step-by-step

  1. Define scope. In your first message, state question, date bounds, regions, industries, and required source tiers (Primary = official/gov/standards; Secondary = reputable analysis; Commentary = opinion).
  2. Plan the searches. Ask ChatGPT to propose 6–10 queries (Boolean, site filters, filetype:pdf). Approve before execution.
  3. Collect & verify. For each result, instruct ChatGPT to open, extract title, author, publisher, publication date, and quote the supporting passage (≤25 words).
  4. Triangulate. Ask ChatGPT for disconfirming evidence and alternative views; log contradictions and who will resolve them.
  5. Synthesize. Have ChatGPT draft a digest with citations inline and a conflicts section. You keep the research log and transcript link in your folder.

Output contract

Each browsing session must produce:

  • Source table: URL, title, author, publisher, publish date, jurisdiction, one-line summary, confidence.
  • Evidence notes: Key stats or short quotes with citation IDs.
  • Freshness tag: News (<30 days), Recent (≤12 months), or Evergreen.
  • Conflict log: Contradictory findings and next verification step.
  • Next actions: Follow-up queries or SME interviews.

Browsing primer (paste first)

Research mode:
- Search widely and include diverse, reputable sources (gov/standards/academia/major outlets).
- For every source list: Title, Author, Publisher, Publication date, Jurisdiction, URL.
- Cite inline like [SRC-01] and add a short supporting quote (≤25 words).
- Mark each source as News (<30d), Recent (≤12mo), or Evergreen.
- If claims conflict, surface both and start a conflict log entry.

Verification prompt (run after collection)

Re-open each source in the table. For every row:
- Confirm author and publication date exist and match the summary.
- Extract a ≤25-word supporting quote with page/section.
- Flag conflicts, paywalls, or dead links and suggest substitutes.
Return a table: Source ID | Passed (Y/N) | Issues | Suggested action.

Disconfirming evidence prompt

List reputable sources that contradict the prevailing claim above.
Summarize each counterpoint in one sentence with author/date and tag it [COUNTER].

Logging

Store the research log in your project folder alongside any downloaded PDFs or screenshots. Capture who found each source, last verification date, and a link to the chat message where ChatGPT listed the citations. For long threads, export or copy the transcript link into the folder README.

Metrics that matter

  • Verification compliance: % of sources with reviewer sign-off.
  • Freshness score: Share of News/Recent vs Evergreen sources.
  • Conflict resolution time: Hours from contradiction to resolved note.

Tips & pitfalls

  • Tip: Save the research log template in your project folder so every browsing session lands in the same format.
  • Tip: After collecting sources, rerun a “diversify results” prompt to force at least one counterpoint per major claim.
  • Pitfall: Shipping paywalled or dead links. Always capture an alternate source or archived copy and note the swap in your log.
  • Pitfall: Letting freshness lapse. Add a final check 24 hours pre-publication to re-verify high-risk stats.

Recap

Scope the question, plan diversified searches, verify every claim with author/date, log conflicts, and archive your trail. With a tight loop, ChatGPT browsing produces research you can stand behind.

Acceptance checklist

  • ✅ Search planner approved and executed in a browsing-enabled chat.
  • ✅ Research log populated with author/date and short quotes.
  • ✅ Verification prompt run; conflicts logged with next actions.
  • ✅ Freshness and coverage reviewed before publication.
  • ✅ Transcript link and artifacts stored in the project folder.
