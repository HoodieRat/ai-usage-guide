Data Analysis Basics

Use ChatGPT as an exploratory data partner—summarize tables, chart trends, and surface anomalies while keeping results auditable.

Bring sample data: Export sanitized CSV snippets or describe column types so ChatGPT can reason without ever touching production secrets.

Data governance: Follow your retention policy. Never upload regulated datasets. Use row sampling or synthetic data when sharing live structures.

What you’ll learn

  • Framing a clear analysis question with business context and hypotheses.
  • Sharing tabular data safely and extracting descriptive statistics.
  • Producing charts and tables that are reproducible in your analytics stack.
  • Capturing prompts, outputs, and follow-up questions in an analysis log.

Prerequisites

  • Access to sanitized sample data or aggregates suitable for sharing.
  • Clarity on your analytics tooling (SQL engine, BI tool, notebook).
  • Defined success criteria—metrics or segments you need to evaluate.

Step-by-step

  1. Define the decision. Document the business question, timeframe, audience, and what “actionable insight” would look like.
  2. Prep the data. Provide column descriptions, units, and a small representative sample. Redact identifiers. Share summary metrics if raw rows are sensitive.
  3. Prime ChatGPT. Paste the question, data context, and preferred output format (table, bullet recap, chart spec). Ask it to confirm assumptions before analyzing.
  4. Request exploratory views. Ask for descriptive statistics, segment comparisons, and outlier checks. Require the model to show formulas or SQL/Pandas equivalents.
  5. Validate externally. Re-run suggested queries or code in your analytics environment. Compare outputs and feed discrepancies back to ChatGPT for reconciliation.
  6. Iterate & visualize. Request chart-ready specifications (vocab, axes, annotations). Convert the spec into your BI tool or notebook, then capture screenshots or embeds.
  7. Document findings. Summarize key insights, caveats, and next steps. Store the prompt/response transcript with links to validated queries and charts.

Validation checklist

- [ ] Prompts and outputs saved in analysis log
- [ ] Summary statistics reproduced in SQL/Pandas
- [ ] Charts rebuilt in BI/notebook and exported
- [ ] Caveats documented (sampling, missing data, bias)
- [ ] Stakeholder sign-off recorded

Output contract

  • Results include clear metric definitions and units.
  • Charts delivered as editable code or spec (Vega-Lite, Plotly, matplotlib).
  • Any assumptions or data quality warnings are listed explicitly.
  • Actionable recommendations mapped to responsible owners.

Metrics that matter

  • Time-to-insight: Hours from data handoff to stakeholder-ready summary.
  • Reproducibility rate: Percentage of ChatGPT-derived findings reproduced in production tooling.
  • Escalation count: Number of governance exceptions raised per analysis cycle.

Tips & pitfalls

  • Ground in context: Restate business definitions (active user, revenue) to avoid misaligned calculations.
  • Limit sample size: Share 10–50 rows max; rely on aggregates for sensitive measures.
  • Ask for reproducible code: Insist on SQL or notebook-ready code so you can verify every number.
  • Track follow-ups: Maintain an analysis log with open questions and pending data pulls.

Recap

Great AI-assisted analysis starts with a crisp brief, clean sample data, and disciplined validation in your source-of-truth tools.

Acceptance checklist

  • ✅ Analysis briefing completed and shared.
  • ✅ Key stats verified in the analytics environment.
  • ✅ Charts recreated and stored in the team library.
  • ✅ Insights, assumptions, and next actions logged.
