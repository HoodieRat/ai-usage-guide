Templates & Integrations

Templates Library

Curate reusable prompt briefs, checklists, and runbooks that any teammate can deploy safely without reinventing guardrails.

Related: Store context in Knowledge Bases, track changes in Version Control for Prompts, and schedule reviews via Automations & Reminders.

Compliance: Lock non-editable guardrails and cite policies; see Citations & Attribution and Redaction & Sanitization.

What you’ll learn

  • Choosing which templates qualify for the canonical library.
  • Embedding self-explanatory metadata and examples.
  • Operating a review cadence to keep content fresh.

Prerequisites

  • Version-controlled repository with permissions and labels.
  • Submission form capturing owner, risk tier, examples, and next review date.
  • Evaluation checklist for quality and compliance.

Step-by-step

  1. Define inclusion criteria. Required sections, risk tier, and sample coverage.
  2. Collect submissions. Use a form that outputs front matter + links.
  3. Quality & compliance review. Lock guardrails; mark customization zones.
  4. Publish & tag. Add to the library with version + change log.
  5. Measure & retire. Track adoption; deprecate stale or low-performing templates.

Output contract

Each template entry includes:

  • Front matter: owner, version, last review, risk tier, tags.
  • Body: purpose, instructions, guardrails, customization fields.
  • Examples: input → output pairs that show quality bar.
  • QA checklist: acceptance criteria for reviewers.

Copy & paste assets

Front matter (YAML)

---
title: Customer Support Triage
owner: support@company.com
version: 1.3
risk: medium
last_review: 2025-10-30
review_cadence_days: 60
tags: [support, triage]
---

Submission form fields

Title | Owner | Risk tier | Purpose (1-2 lines) | Guardrails | Examples (2+) | Next review date

QA checklist

- Guardrails present and locked
- Examples meet tone/format
- Personal data redacted
- Links to policies included

Logging

Track submissions, approvals, version tags, and retirements in a changelog. Link each entry to evaluation results.

Metrics that matter

  • Adoption: unique users or teams using the template.
  • Update freshness: days since last review vs. cadence.
  • Quality signals: reviewer pass rate or incident count.

Tips & pitfalls

  • Curate once: Discourage private forks—link every workflow to the canonical template entry.
  • Highlight editable zones: Use comments or brackets so users know where customization is allowed.
  • Automate freshness: Trigger review reminders ahead of the `review_cadence_days` deadline.
  • Track adoption: Instrument copy/download counts so you can retire low-value templates.

Recap

A managed templates library prevents reinvention and errors. Curate, review, and retire with discipline.

Acceptance checklist

  • ✅ Submission + review workflow operational.
  • ✅ Entries include front matter, examples, and QA checklist.
  • ✅ Changelog and freshness reviews automated.
