Source Quality & Fact Checking

Confirm that the information your AI surfaces is reliable, current, and bias-aware before you rely on it.

As of today (October 30, 2025): Regulators increasingly require auditable fact-check logs. Store your verification steps alongside deliverables. See Citations & Attribution.

What you’ll learn

  • How to assess source credibility (authorship, venue, bias).
  • How to embed lightweight fact-check loops in your workflow.
  • How to document verification so stakeholders trust the findings.

Prerequisites

  • A source-quality rubric or checklist aligned to your policies.
  • A fact-check log template in a shared workspace.
  • Access to primary sources (journals, statutes, official docs) used in your domain.

Planner → Actor → Reviewer walkthrough

Planner prompt

“We’re briefing executives on AI governance trends. Build a verification plan covering each major claim, required sources, and reviewers.”

Actor response

The assistant groups claims by topic, proposes source tiers (primary vs. secondary), and recommends verification tactics for data, quotes, and interpretations. Use Deep Research Playbook.

Reviewer check

Reviewer flags claims that trigger compliance review, adds mandatory databases, and confirms deadlines plus sign-off sequence.

Step-by-step

  1. Inventory claims. Highlight every factual assertion, statistic, quote, and implied causal statement.
  2. Assign verification approach. Map each claim to required source type, verification method, and reviewer.
  3. Collect evidence. Pull the original source, capture citation metadata, and note potential bias indicators (how to cite).
  4. Run cross-checks. Compare the claim across at least two independent sources or datasets; log discrepancies. Use Browsing & Search and News & Recency for freshness.
  5. Document outcomes. Update the fact-check log with status, reviewer initials, and any caveats that must accompany publication.

Output contract

Every deliverable that relies on facts must include:

  • Claim ledger: Table of all claims with verification status (verified, challenged, pending) and reviewer.
  • Source dossier: Links or attachments for every primary source with collection timestamps.
  • Risk annotation: Notes on disputed or low-confidence items and how they are communicated.
  • Audit trail: Location of the fact-check log and evidence snippets for future audits.

Counter-check prompt

List the three most likely ways this claim could be inaccurate:
Claim: {{paste claim}}
Context: {{summarize supporting evidence}}
For each potential issue, suggest a source or method to validate it.

Logging

Capture verification sessions with timestamps, claim IDs reviewed, sources accessed, and reviewer decisions. Store screenshots or downloads of primary sources in the same evidence folder so future audits can replay the verification path.

Metrics that matter

  • Verification coverage: Percentage of claims with completed fact-check entries.
  • Escalation rate: Portion of claims requiring compliance or legal review.
  • Discrepancy resolution time: Average hours to resolve challenged claims.

Tips & pitfalls

  • Tip: Rate each source with the rubric as soon as you capture it; the notes speed up reviewer decisions later.
  • Tip: Log conflicting findings side-by-side so SMEs can resolve them without repeating the search.
  • Pitfall: Publishing without a primary source. If you can’t locate one, mark the claim “unverified” and add an explicit caveat.
  • Pitfall: Letting stats age out. Schedule a recency sweep using News & Recency before final approval.

Recap

Trustworthy AI outputs come from disciplined verification. Log every claim, evaluate sources against a rubric, and resolve discrepancies before decisions are made.

Acceptance checklist

  • ✅ All claims mapped to a verification status with reviewer initials.
  • ✅ Supporting sources archived with metadata and retrieval timestamps.
  • ✅ Risky or unresolved items flagged in the deliverable with mitigation notes.
  • ✅ Fact-check log stored in the team’s evidence repository.
